Open Enrollment 2019 FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When is Open Enrollment?

Open Enrollment is September 10 through October 19, 2018. Your HR representative will provide specific dates and instructions for your location.

Q: How do I complete my enrollment?

Enrollment instructions vary depending on your location. Please see your enrollment packet for details.

Q: Where can I find 2019 benefit and rate information?

Your HR representative will provide a benefits packet with plan information and rates for 2019.


Q: Can I enroll on this website?

No. This website gives Open Enrollment information, similar to the Open Enrollment guide. See your enrollment packet or contact your HR representative for enrollment instructions for your location.

Q: What information do I need for enrollment?

Please have the following information available for your enrollment session:

  • Your Associate ID number and badge
  • Your date of birth and Social Security number
  • Marriage certificates or birth certificates for any dependents with a different last name than yours, as well as Social Security numbers for any dependents you plan to enroll
  • Beneficiary information

Q: What if I speak a language other than English?

If your location will be having onsite enrollers, please check with your local HR Representative to see what languages can be accommodated. Spanish-speaking counselors are usually available, and translators may be provided for some additional languages, depending upon your location. Associates may also bring their own interpreter if they wish. If you are enrolling by phone, the majority of languages can be accommodated.

Q: Who is eligible for benefits?

You are eligible for benefits if you are a full-time associate working 30 or more hours per week and have satisfied your benefits waiting period.

Your eligible dependents may include:


  • Your legal opposite-sex or same-sex spouse (unless you are legally separated)
  • Your children up to age 26, regardless of marital or student status, even if they are eligible for other insurance coverage
  • Your children over age 26 who are physically or mentally disabled may be eligible (see Summary Plan Description for more details)
  • Individuals who are neither your spouse nor child(ren) whereby you have legal guardianship
