Medical

Highmark Blue Cross Blue Shield PPO Plan


This plan is a Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) plan, which generally allows you to see any licensed provider but pays a higher benefit in most cases if a provider is used from the PPO network.

If you go to a non-preferred provider, the plan's payment for a service by that non-preferred provider may be substantially less than the amount billed. You are responsible for the difference between the amount the plan pays and the amount billed for non-preferred providers. It is therefore to your advantage to obtain medical and hospital services from preferred providers.

Note: Some non-network providers have contracts with Highmark Delaware that limit what they may charge. If you visit such a provider, benefits will still be paid at the out-of-network percentage, but the provider will not bill you more than the allowable charge for the covered service. If you go to a non-preferred provider, be sure to ask whether that provider has such a contract with Highmark Delaware.

Note: All plan benefits are subject to possible exclusions and limitations.

Spending Accounts

Healthcare Flexible Spending Account

The Healthcare Flexible Spending Account is a tax-free account that allows you to pay for healthcare expenses that are not covered, or are partially covered, by your medical, dental and vision insurance plans. This may include deductibles, co-payments and co-insurance. By contributing a portion of your payroll dollars into your Flexible Spending Account on a pre-tax basis, you can save on the cost of eligible expenses you are already incurring.

When you enroll in a Flexible Spending Account, you decide how much to contribute for the entire Plan Year. The money is then deducted from your paycheck, pre-tax (before Federal & State income taxes and FICA taxes are deducted) in equal amounts over the course of the plan year. After you incur expenses that qualify for reimbursement, you submit claims (reimbursement requests) to request tax-free withdrawals from your Flexible Spending Account to reimburse yourself for these expenses.

If you elect a Healthcare Flexible Spending Account, you will automatically receive a Your Spending Account debit card. The Your Spending Account card can be used to pay for health-related expenses quickly and easily, and is pre-loaded with your election for the year. You may use this debit card to pay for eligible expenses at the doctor’s office, pharmacy and other medical service locations.

You may contribute up to $2,600 for healthcare expenses each calendar year. At any time during the year, you can receive reimbursement from your Healthcare Flexible Spending Account for the full amount you elected.

Participants in the Healthcare Flexible Spending account may carry over up to $500 from their accounts into the following calendar year. The carryover funds may be used towards reimbursement of eligible claims in the following calendar year.

Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account

The Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account reimburses you for the expenses of day care, nursery school or babysitting that enable you and/or your spouse to work.

In order to be reimbursed for dependent care expenses, your dependent must meet one of the following categories:

  • Dependent child(ren) under 13 years old
  • Dependent adults who meet the IRS definition of dependency and regularly spend at least eight hours a day at home with you
  • Dependent who is physically or mentally unable to care for himself or herself

When you elect this benefit, you estimate what your total dependent care expenses will be for the calendar and determine the amount that will be deposited in the account.


Life Insurance

Basic Life Insurance

Gore provides associates with life insurance and accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D) insurance in an amount equal to their annual base pay (minimum of $10,000 and a maximum of $250,000). This benefit is offered by Unum Life Insurance Company. You are now able to designate separate beneficiaries for your Basic Life and Supplemental Life through BenefitSource™ online.

Supplemental Life Insurance

Gore offers Supplemental Life coverage through Unum Life Insurance Company of America. Associates may choose additional life insurance equal to 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 times their salary up to a combined maximum (basic and supplemental) of $1,500,000. If you choose supplemental life insurance, it will be added to the Gore-provided basic life insurance (1 x annual pay). If you choose coverage that exceeds 3 times your annual earnings or over $750,000, you will need to provide evidence of insurability (EOI) to Unum.

Spouse and Domestic Partner Life Insurance

Gore offers Spouse & Domestic Partner Life Insurance coverage through Unum Life Insurance Company of America. This plan provides three levels of life insurance coverage for your spouse or domestic partner. The cost for this coverage is a flat rate based on the amount of coverage.

Child Life Insurance

Gore offers Child Life coverage through Unum Life Insurance Company of America. This plan provides three levels of life insurance coverage for your dependent children. The cost for this coverage is a flat rate based on the amount of coverage. Coverage is for each dependent child who is under age 26 and older than two weeks. Coverage for children less than 2 weeks old is $1,000. Stillborn children are not eligible. Please Note: Once your children reach age 26, they are no longer eligible for this life insurance benefit. You are responsible for declaring a life event to terminate your Child Life election. Please contact the BenefitSource™ at 1-866-641-5595 to document the life event and to learn about the various options to continue this benefit outside of Gore.

Retirement & Savings

401(k)

Gore provides a 401(k) contribution of up to 3% of pay for associates' personal investment accounts. This "gift" is given regardless of whether the associate contributes or not. You will be automatically enrolled in the Plan at an Associate contribution rate of 3% unless you decide to opt out.

The plan also allows associates to make pre-tax and/or after-tax contributions of up to 100% of their eligible pay (up to IRS annual limits) through payroll deduction. The IRS sets annual limits on the amount you can defer. These limits change annually.

You begin participating in the plan after one month of service. With the first full pay after the one-month mark, the Gore, and your Associate contribution begins and you may start making contributions from your pay. You are immediately vested in the money in your accounts. If you have been rehired by Gore and met the plan’s eligibility requirements during your previous employment period, you will be immediately eligible to participate in the plan.

Other Benefits

Parental Leave

Associates working 20 or more hours per week are eligible for up to six (6) weeks of paid time off to bond with a new child (under age 18) entering the family through birth, adoption, surrogacy, or foster care placement. Leave must be taken consecutively within 12 months of the parenting event.

f eligible, an Associate may pursue Caregiver Leave to extend the bonding period for a maximum of 10 weeks of paid time off per event. The duration of leave shall not be affected by multiple births or adoption of more than one child at a time.

Parental Leave must be taken continuously. It can start on any day of the week, but once leave starts, the total entitlement must be taken without interruption. If an Associate returns to work before the leave entitlement has been exhausted, the remainder of leave will be forfeited. Payment for leave is in accordance with Unum's approval dates. Any period of leave outside of Unum's approval period would be covered by an Associate's personal sick, vacation or unpaid time (with leadership approval).

Parental Leave will run concurrently with any applicable federal and/or state leave programs for a maximum of 100% pay. To initiate leave, first inform your leader, then contact Unum (8000-532-9590) and HR Services (1-877-942-7547).

Caregiver Leave

Associates working 20 or more hours per week with at least 90 days of service may take up to four (4) weeks of paid time off per calendar year to care for a seriously ill child, parent, parent-in-law, grandparent, grandchild, sibling, spouse, or registered domestic partner. Leave benefits are also available to parents who need time to bond with a new child entering their family by birth, adoption, surrogacy or foster care placement. If the benefit is used to bond with a new child, no additional leave time will be granted under Caregiver Leave for an Associate to care for a seriously family member until the next calendar year.

Caregiver Leave must be taken continuously. It can start on any day of the week, but once leave starts, the total entitlement must be taken without interruption. If an Associate returns to work before the leave entitlement has been exhausted, the remainder of leave will be forfeited. Any period of leave outside of Unum’s approval period would be covered by an Associate’s personal sick, vacation or unpaid time (with leadership approval). Caregiver Leave will run concurrently with any applicable federal and/or state leave programs for a maximum of 100% pay. To initiate leave, first inform your leader, then contact Unum (8000-532-9590) and HR Services (1-877-942-7547).

Holidays

Gore offers paid holidays each year. Each year's schedule depends upon when major holidays fall during the work week. The schedule always contains the following holidays:

  • New Year's Day
  • Martin Luther King Jr. Day
  • Good Friday
  • Memorial Day
  • Independence Day
  • Labor Day
  • Thanksgiving Day
  • The day after Thanksgiving Day
  • Christmas Day
  • Additional supplemental days as appropriate

Gore provides a floating holiday to regular full and part time Associates to be taken at the Associate’s discretion any time during the calendar year. The number of hours given for the floating holiday are as follows:

  • If your standard work week is five 8 hour shifts, you receive 8 hours.
  • If your standard work week is four 10 hours shifts, you receive 10 hours.
  • If your standard work week is three 12 hour shifts, you receive 13.33 hours.
  • If your standard work week is four 9 hour shifts and one 4 hour shift, you receive 9 hours. NOTE: if you take your floating holiday on the day you normally work 4 hours, you must report 4 hours of floating holiday time, and you forfeit the other 5 hours.
  • For Part-Time Associates, the hours of the floating holiday are pro-rated based upon the number of hours worked in a standard work week (not shift) – (i.e. for a standard 30 hour work week your floating holiday would be equivalent to 6 hours).

You may schedule the floating holiday in advance at any time during the calendar year with notice to your leader. The floating holiday, which must be taken as a whole day, will be lost at the end of the year if not used by December 31st. Gore Temporary Associates are not eligible for the floating holiday. Associates hired after July 1 will not receive the floating holiday for that calendar year.

Associates who work both the day before and the day after a holiday are eligible for holiday pay. Exception to this guideline which will also entitle the Associate to holiday pay is when the absence is excused (i.e. Time Off Benefits or Protected Leaves of Absence).

Long Term Disability

Unum is the carrier for the Long Term Disability (LTD) plan.

This plan offers you continuing income if you are unable to work because of a disability. All associates are provided LTD coverage. The Gore-paid benefit is 50% of base salary with an associate voluntary buy up option of 20% of base salary. Total LTD coverage could be 70%. Benefits are reduced dollar for dollar by any disability benefits received from Social Security (family benefits), any state disability plan, workers' compensation, etc. The maximum combined benefit amount is $14,500 per month. The minimum monthly benefit amount is $100 or 10% of your gross disability payment.

LTD benefits begin after 26 weeks of eligible continuous disability. In general, benefits may continue until you return to work, no longer qualify for disability, reach maximum period of payment, or die. The 50% Gore paid benefit is a pretax benefit and is treated as taxable income. The 20% voluntary buy up is purchased with after-tax dollars and therefore reduces your tax liability if you become disabled and have to collect this benefit.

If your salary increases during the calendar year, your long term disability coverage volume and per pay cost increase as well.

There is an elimination period of 180 days before the plan will pay benefits.

You would be considered disabled and eligible for benefits when Unum determines that:

  • You are limited from performing the material and substantial duties of your regular occupation due to your sickness or injury; and
  • You have a 20% or more loss in your indexed monthly earnings due to the same sickness or injury.

After 24 months of payments, you are disabled when Unum determines that due to the same sickness or injury, you are unable to perform the duties of any gainful occupation for which you are reasonably fitted by education, training or experience.

The loss of a professional or occupational license or certification does not, in itself, constitute disability. You must be under the regular care of a physician in order to be considered disabled.

Note: The plan may create a rehabilitation program to help return you to work. Failure to work with the prescribed program could result in loss of benefits. Contact Unum Customer Service for more information.

Other services available from Unum as a part of your plan include:

  • A worksite modification program — to help you perform the material and substantial duties of your regular occupation with your employer.
  • A Social Security claimant advocacy program — this can provide expert advice regarding your claim and assist you with your application and appeal.
Limitations/Reduction of Benefits

There could be limitations or a reduction to the amount of benefits you are eligible to receive. This could be caused by a pre-existing condition.

Unum has determined a pre-existing condition is anything treated in the three (3) months immediately prior to joining the plan (known as the look-back period).

A pre-existing condition will not be covered unless a disability caused by it begins at least 12 months after coverage began for the individual.

There is a special limitation for conditions that are sometimes called "self-reported."

Such conditions in the insurance industry generally include symptoms that cannot be verified using tests or clinical examinations that are accepted as standard medical practice. Some examples of self-reported conditions could be unverifiable pain, fatigue, or loss of energy. Payments for such disabilities under the plan, if approved will be limited to up to 24 months.
