Critical Illness Insurance

A serious illness can strike at any time. Aside from the usual anxiety, you have to deal with the financial impact as well. The fees for doctors, tests, hospital stays, and other services add up quickly, leaving many people to rely on their savings and credit cards.

Critical Illness Insurance helps provide financial protection in the event of a covered serious illness.

The way it works is simple. The policy pays the benefit directly to you if you are diagnosed with a covered condition. You can use this benefit any way you choose – to pay deductibles and coinsurance, to cover expenses your family incurs to be by your side, or simply to replace your lost earnings from being out of work.

CRITICAL ILLNESSES INCLUDE:

  • Heart attack
  • Blindness
  • Major organ failure
  • End-stage kidney failure
  • Benign brain tumor
  • Coronary artery bypass surgery
  • Coma that lasts at least 14 consecutive days
  • Stroke whose effects are confirmed at least 30 days after
  • Occupational HIV
  • Permanent paralysis of at least two limbs due to a covered accident
  • Carcinoma in Situ**
  • Cancer

*American Association for Critical Illness Insurance, “The Real Risk That You’ll Have A Critical Illness,” September 2010. **You receive 25% of the full benefit for these conditions.

PLAN FEATURES:

  • You can elect to cover your spouse and children.
  • The cost is conveniently deducted from your paycheck.
  • Coverage is individually owned, which means you can take your coverage with you if you change jobs or retire.
  • Every year, each covered family member can receive $50 for getting a routine health screening test.
  • You’re guaranteed coverage without having to answer health questions.
  • Rates are based on age and benefit amount and are shown during the enrollment process.
