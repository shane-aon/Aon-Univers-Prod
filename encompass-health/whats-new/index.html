Medical Plan Changes and Benefits Program Enhancements

This year, we have added a third plan to better provide a variety of options for our employees. For 2018, a high-deductible health plan (HDHP) will be offered, which carries a higher annual deductible, but lower annual premiums. This is paired with a Health Savings Account (HSA) to help cover out-of-pocket expenses until the deductible is met. The cost of the Core Medical plan remains the same for 2018; however, the Plus plan premiums will increase slightly. Please see rates for the three plans below.

HSA Bank If you participate in the HDHP medical plan, you are eligible to enroll in a Health Savings Account – also referred to as an HSA. Contributions into an HSA account are deducted from your paycheck before taxes and can be used to pay for current and future healthcare expenses. Unused funds will roll over year to year – there is no “use it or lose it” rule, and if you leave your job, your HSA account and the money in it goes with you.

To be eligible:
  • you must be covered under the HDHP medical plan
  • you may not be enrolled in Medicare nor Tricare
  • you may not be claimed as a dependent on someone else’s tax return
  • you may not have received VA Benefits within the last 3 months of eligibility

Once enrolled:
  • HSA Flyer with FAQ on back
  • Welcome letter
  • Deposit agreement and disclosures
  • Privacy and Opt Out Notice
  • Fee schedule

IRS Regulations To Transition From the CYC Medical FSA to the New HSA Plan (if you elect the HDHP Medical Plan):

-If you participated in the Connect Your Care medical FSA in 2017, and there are any unused funds in your medical FSA account on 12/31/2017, you will automatically be placed in an FSA Grace Period until March 15th. This Grace Period allows you to use your unused funds from 2017 to pay for eligible expenses incurred between January 1 - March 15, 2018.

-The IRS does not allow anyone in a FSA Grace Period to start an HSA enrollment until April 1, 2018 (first of the month after the Grace Period ends) - even if you use these funds before March 15th.

-This waiting period applies to both the employer and employee contributions. -If this situation applies to you, your HSA election and contributions will start on April 1, 2018.

-An employee who enrolls in the HDHP and elects an HSA account, Encompass Health will contribute up to $500 ($19.23/paycheck) towards your HSA plan to help with your out-of-pocket expenses.
