PREVENTING DIABETES

Posted on April 2019

According to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control, “In the United States, 84.1 million adults—more than 1 in 3—have prediabetes. What’s more, 90% of them don’t know they have it. With prediabetes, blood sugar levels are higher than normal, but not high enough yet to be diagnosed as type 2 diabetes. Prediabetes raises your risk for type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and stroke.”

Click here to learn more about Prediabetes and, if you are at risk for Diabetes, what HBC is doing to help you take healthy steps to reverse your risk.

COLORECTAL CANCER PREVENTION

Posted on April 2019

Colorectal cancer is the third most common cancer in men and women and is the second leading cause of death from cancer in the United States. The number of new colorectal cancer cases and the number of deaths from colorectal cancer are both decreasing a little bit each year in adults aged 55 and older. However, in adults younger than 55 years, the number of new colorectal cancer cases has slowly increased in recent years. [source: Division of Cancer Prevention and Control, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention).

Click here to learn the steps you can take to minimize your risk.
