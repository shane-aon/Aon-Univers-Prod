Depression Awareness and Screening

Posted on October 2018

Do you feel sad, empty, and hopeless most of the day, nearly every day? Have you lost interest or pleasure in your hobbies or being with friends and family? Are you having trouble sleeping, eating, and functioning? If you have felt this way for two weeks or more, you may have depression, a serious but treatable mood disorder.


Here are some recommended ways to seek help:


Take a quick, confidential, online depression screening test through either the Mental Health America or HBC’s Optum EAP. Note: for the Optum EAP, type in Access Code “HBC”, then Search “Depression Screener”.

Speak with a professional at HBC’s Optum EAP by calling 866-248-4094. As an employee of HBC, you can have UNLIMITED telephone consultations and up to five in-person consultations per issue at NO CHARGE

Use one of the below resources to find an outside health provider:


  • HBC’s Optum EAP at 866-248-4094 or webpage at https://www.liveandworkwell.com/content/en/public.html.
  • National Institute for Mental Health “Help for Mental Illnesses” webpage at www.nimh.nih.gov/findhelp.
  • he Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) webpage at https://findtreatment.samhsa.gov/.
  • Your local community center
  • Your local mental health association
  • Your health insurance company
  • Your personal physician or other healthcare professional

If you or someone you know is in crisis, you can get help quickly:
  • Call 911 for emergency services.
  • Call the toll-free, 24-hour hotline of the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-TALK (1-800-273-8255); TYY: 1-800-799-4TTY (4889).
  • Call HBC’s Optum EAP at 866-248-4094 to speak with a mental health professional 24/7.
  • Call your or your loved one’s personal physician or another health professional.
  • If all else fails, go to the nearest hospital emergency room.
