Adoption Assistance

Posted on November 2018

The HBC Adoption Assistance Program reimburses up to $5,000 of covered expenses per child, for up to two children. Full-time and part-time Associates scheduled to work 20 or more hours per week with at least one year of service are eligible for Adoption benefits. HBC will reimburse you, up to the plan limit, for the following covered expenses:


  • Licensed adoption agency and placement fees (including foreign adoption)

  • Legal fees and court fees

  • Transportation and lodging costs to bring the child to the adoptive parents

  • Immigration, immunization and translation fees

Expenses that are Not Covered include:


  • Expenses related to the adoption of your spouse’s child

  • Any adoption that results in a violation of federal or state law

  • A surrogate parenting arrangement (see Surrogacy benefit)

  • Medical expenses of the birth mother or child prior to the adoption

  • Temporary foster care homes

  • Fees for services not yet performed

HOW TO REQUEST REIMBURSEMENT


Once the adoption is final, complete an Adoption Assistance Claim Form and return it to the HBC Benefits Department, 250 Highland Park Blvd. Wilkes-Barre, PA 18702. Include itemized receipts for expenses listed and proof of adoption.


IF YOU HAVE QUESTIONS call 1-877-725-7238

SURROGACY ASSISTANCE

The HBC Surrogacy Assistance Program reimburses up to $5,000 of covered expenses per child to the “intended parents” for up to two legal Surrogacy arrangements, whether traditional or gestational.

Full-time and part-time Associates scheduled to work 20 or more hours per week with at least one year of service who are “intended parents” are eligible for Surrogacy benefits.

HBC will reimburse you, up to the plan limit, for the following covered expenses:


  • Agency Fee
  • Surrogate Search
  • Surrogate's Pay
  • Legal Costs
  • Clinical Fees
  • Prenatal Care
  • Immigration Help
  • Legal fees and court fees

Surrogacy laws differ from state to state and from country to country. Surrogacy is considered “against public policy” and surrogacy contracts are currently not enforceable in Michigan, New York and Washington. Some states have no laws regarding surrogacy and other states vary in their legal treatment of surrogacy and birth rights. You should consult your personal legal counsel or surrogacy agency for more information, or go to Surrogate.com for more information.


HOW TO REQUEST REIMBURSEMENT


Once the Surrogacy birth is final, complete a Surrogacy Assistance Claim Form and return it to the HBC Benefits Department, 250 Highland Park Blvd. Wilkes-Barre, PA 18702. Include itemized receipts for expenses listed and proof of surrogacy.

Forms can be obtained on the myHBCbenefits.com website.


IF YOU HAVE QUESTIONS call 1-877-725-7238
