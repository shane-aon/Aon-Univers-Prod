Diabetes Awareness

Posted on November 2018

Did you know that 1 in 10 U.S. adults over the age of 18 has diabetes, and an estimated 85 million people have pre-diabetes? And nearly 25% of adults who have Diabetes don’t know they have it. Diabetes is a leading cause of heart attack, stroke, blindness, kidney failure, and amputation. The good news is, type 2 diabetes is preventable. Click below to learn more

Healthy Skin

Posted on November 2018

Did you know that your skin is your body’s largest organ? The average adult has approximately 16 to 22 square feet of skin weighing between 9 – 11 pounds – about 15% of your body weight. The American Academy of Dermatology has designated November as National Healthy Skin Month. Click below for more information on your skin and tips on how to keep it healthy.

Quit Smoking and Reduce Your Lung Cancer Risk?

Posted on November 2018

According to the Centers for Disease Control, cigarette smoking is the number one risk factor for lung cancer. In the United States, cigarette smoking is linked to about 80% to 90% of lung cancer deaths. Using other tobacco products such as cigars or pipes also increases the risk for lung cancer. Quitting at any age can lower your risk of having lung cancer compared to not quitting. Click below to learn more.
